Proposal Details
Brief Description of the Study
  1. * In 3 sentences summarize the overall aims of your study. How will MR contribute to your study?
Details of animals used: * If one of the required fields does not apply, please enter N/A
  1. *What species of animal(s) will be used?
  2. *How many animals of each species will undergo MR?
  3. *What anesthesia will be used? ( the center provides isoflurane anesthesia).
  4. *Describe how the animals being imaged have been altered (e.g. genetically altered, stroke model, tumor model etc), and if this will affect their ability to survive anesthesia?
Details of proposed MR:
  1. *What part of the animal do you need to image (e.g. brain, spine, body fat, whole body etc)
  2. *What resolution (imaging) or voxel size (spectroscopy) is required to address your scientific question? Example: A typical high-resolution anatomical scan of a mouse brain would provide voxels with an in-plane resolution of 80 microns and a through plane resolution of 300 microns. (e.g. a typical voxel size is 80 by 80 by 300 microns). The quality of the image will depend on the scan time. For more information please email Dr. Miriam Scadeng at mscadeng@ucsd.edu
  3. *How long do you expect the entire project to last?
  4. *How many imaging sessions will you expect to need?
  5. *Does this study involve repeated imaging in the same animal (longitudinal studies), please list the proposed time points?
  6. *Will you need to inject any compounds during the MR scan (e.g. contrast agents, gene induction agents.)? If so, give details of route of injection (intra-venous, intra-arterial, intra-peritoneal etc and at what time point during the scan e.g. prior to scan, at start of scan half way through etc.)
  7. * Does this study require repeat imaging at fixed time points (e.g. control, 12 hrs, 24 hrs, etc). Is this an acute study, or survival study? Please note animals cannot be euthanised at the fMRI center.
  8. * Does your study conform to the Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for animal MRI at the fMRI center? If not, please detail what additional procedures will be done. Note: An SOP is currently available for standard imaging studies of rodents.
